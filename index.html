How to actually get the six-pack

The deficit does the reveal

You already have abs under there. Training thickens them; a calorie deficit uncovers them. Aim ~300–500 kcal/day below maintenance. Slow loss (~0.5 kg/week) protects the muscle you're building.

Protein is non-negotiable

~1.6–2.0 g/kg/day — roughly 150–180 g for you. It preserves muscle in a deficit and keeps you full. Spread it across 3–4 meals.

Why this won't make you bulky

Bulk comes from a heavy surplus and very high volume over years. You're in a deficit with moderate volume — the result is hard, lean, defined muscle, not mass.

Progressive overload, simply

Hit the top of the rep range with 1–2 reps left in the tank? Add a little weight next session. If you can't hit the bottom of the range, drop weight slightly. That's the whole game.

Weights are starting guesses

The numbers shown are estimates for your bodyweight. Calibrate to your last-2-reps-are-hard point in week one, then climb from there.

Warm up, every day

5 min treadmill walk + 1–2 light feeler sets on your first big lift. Cold heavy presses are how shoulders and lower backs get hurt.

Built as a reference — not a tracker. Calibrate loads to your own effort. If a movement causes joint pain (not muscle burn), swap it. Reassess the whole plan every 8–10 weeks.
